Alexander Hamilton

US Secretary of Treasury & Cultural Icon

The Ten Dollar Foudning Father

Life & Career Oversimplified

  • Immigrates to New York at 15-years-old in 1772 to attend King's College
  • Joins the Revolutionary War effort in 1775 and is on George Washington's staff by 1777
  • In 1780 he marries Elizabeth (Eliza) Schuyler and goes on to have 8 kids, two named Philip
  • Joins Congress in 1782
  • Starts practicing law in 1783
  • The Constitution is written by James Madison in 1787, of which Hamilton is a fierce supporter
  • Hamilton's efforts prevail and in 1788 the Constitution is ratified
  • Washington appoints Hamilton the first Secretary of Treasury in 1789
  • Successfully establishes The National Bank in 1791
  • Hamilton publishes The Reynolds Pamphlet in 1997 to preemptively expose an affair his political opponents had become privvy to
  • Aaron Burr challenges Hamilton to a duel in 1804 for having not endorsed him in the 1800 election, and kills Hamilton

A Legacy Makes Bank

  • Ron Chernow publishes a biography on Alexander Hamilton in 2004
  • Lin-Manuel Miranda buys a copy of the book to read on a vacation in 2008
  • The first song of the Hamilton concept album is reveal at a 2009 White House music gala
  • A part of the final musical is performed in 2013 at a Vassar College workshop
  • Hamilton debuts on Broadway in 2015
  • Hamilton goes back on tour in 2019 through Puerto Rico as a fundrasing effort after Hurricane Maria
  • The musical is released on Disney+ in July, 2020 for a wider audience to experience
